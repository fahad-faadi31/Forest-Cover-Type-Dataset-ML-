Why Random Forest as the 4th Algorithm?

Random Forest is an ensemble method not covered in class. It builds multiple decision trees and aggregates their votes, making it highly resistant to overfitting. On this dataset's high-dimensional mix of continuous terrain features and binary soil/wilderness indicators, it consistently outperforms KNN and Naïve Bayes by leveraging feature importance across 100 trees.

Improving Naïve Bayes Results

Naïve Bayes underperforms here because it assumes feature independence — but elevation, slope, and hillshade are strongly correlated. To improve: apply PCA first to decorrelate features, or use Complement Naïve Bayes which handles imbalanced multi-class problems better. Feature engineering (combining hillshade values into a single index) can also help separate overlapping classes.
